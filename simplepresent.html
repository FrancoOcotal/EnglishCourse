Simple Present Tense

The simple present tense is used to describe actions that are habitual, regular, or generally true.

Rules for Simple Present Tense:

  • For regular actions or habits:
    Example: "She reads books every night."
  • For facts or things that are always true:
    Example: "The sun rises in the east."
  • For scheduled events in the future:
    Example: "The train leaves at 9 AM tomorrow."

Structure:

Positive Sentences: Subject + base form of the verb (s/es for third person singular)

Negative Sentences: Subject + do/does not + base form of the verb

Questions: Do/Does + subject + base form of the verb?

Simple Present Tense - Adding 'es'

In the simple present tense, verbs in the third person singular (he, she, it, or a singular noun) usually take 'es' at the end.

Rules:

For verbs ending in '-o', '-s', '-ss', '-sh', '-ch', '-x', we add 'es' for the third person singular.

Examples:

  • He goes to the gym every morning.
  • She passes the ball skillfully.
  • It washes the dishes after dinner.
  • The fox catches its prey.
  • He fixes the car when it's broken.

Simple Present Tense - Verbs Ending in 'Consonant + y'

In the simple present tense, for verbs ending in 'consonant + y', we change 'y' to 'i' and add 'es' for the third person singular.

Examples:

  • She tries a new recipe every weekend.
  • He studies diligently for his exams.
  • The company carries out regular audits.
  • It flies gracefully in the sky.
  • She plies her trade expertly.
